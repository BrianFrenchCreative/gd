2026 Chicago Auto Show

Experiential Branding & Spatial Design

I. Chi-Town Alley

I had the honor of leading the environmental graphics and spatial branding to define the visual identity for Chi-Town Alley, a new platform dedicated to the city's homegrown car community at the 2026 Chicago Auto Show.

  • 🎯 The Challenge

    The Chicago Auto Show needed to convert a legacy commercial layout into a dynamic, community-first event space. Starting with nothing more than an existing logo and a high-level goal for the zone, my challenge was to build out a complete, authentic visual identity system. This environmental branding had to scale across massive three-dimensional architectural structures while remaining versatile enough to frame interactive zones, retail booths, and vehicle displays without causing visual clutter.

  • 👤 My Role

    Lead Experiential Graphic Designer / Art Director

    Conceptual Art Direction, Spatial Visualization, End-to-End Asset Production, Creative & Production Alignment

  • ⚙️ My Process
    • Concept Development: Developed the initial concepts for the main entry towers to establish the foundational look and feel of the space.
    • Collaboration & Alignment with Leadership: When project leadership introduced new brand constraints requiring a shift away from the initial color palette, I quickly pivoted the design. I adapted the environmental graphics to align perfectly with their updated standards without losing the high-energy aesthetic of the space.
    • Agile Adaptation: As the environment took shape, the strength of the initial design inspired leadership to expand the scope and invest in a massive overhead ring as a centerpiece for the exhibit. Tasked with this late structural addition, I researched the physical medium and built high-fidelity mockups, using advanced photo-compositing to simulate the graphics within the physical space. I successfully secured executive sign-off on the ring and delivered final, press-ready files — all within a seamless 7-day sprint.
    • Scalable Asset Production: With the branding and structural elements fully approved, I managed a comprehensive production workflow, scaling the visual identity down to informational meterboards and promotional touchpoints.

  • 📈 The Outcome

    Thanks to the early alignment we built, the final designs earned immediate sign-off from project leadership and the executive production team. The resulting space generated strong organic visibility across social media as attendees and enthusiasts documented the exhibit. Ultimately, because the production team was thrilled with the seamless workflow and final execution, I was asked back to lead an expansion of the spatial branding for the upcoming 2027 show cycle.

II. Chicago Overlanding

Transforming standard event collateral into authentic brand touchpoints

For the Overlanding Chicago footprint, my initial brief was simply to update standard informational elements like the vehicle placards and the participating exhibitor list. Recognizing an opportunity to elevate the space, I pitched a reimagined concept that transformed these basic templates into highly themed physical artifacts. Standard vehicle specs became custom, commemorative license plates, while the traditional sponsor directory was built out as a massive, freestanding park-style sign featuring weathered typography and corkboard aesthetics. To further anchor the immersive environment, I also designed authentic trailhead signage featuring topographic maps and technical trail etiquette for the Midwest Driftless Area. By treating every operational requirement as an opportunity for world-building, we delivered a space that perfectly captured the rugged personality of the off-road community.

III. Supercar Gallery

Navigating high-profile stakeholders with premium touchpoints and strategic design

For the Drive Chicago Supercar Gallery, the primary challenge was managing a notoriously discerning group of participating luxury dealers who were dissatisfied with the previous year's collateral. To navigate their expectations and foster a sense of ownership, I strategically presented six unique typographic directions for the vehicle placards. Giving them a voice in the process accelerated the approval timeline, resulting in a clean sign-off on a highly elegant, premium design that we paired with upgraded physical stands. The project also required rapid, agile execution; when a major dealer unexpectedly pulled out just before the show, I seamlessly executed last-minute revisions to the primary directory signage to ensure the gallery's presentation remained flawless.

IV. Experiential Touchpoints

Scaling the visual identity across partnerships and interactive activations

Maintaining total visual immersion requires extending the core aesthetic into every corner of the event space, including sponsor partnerships and interactive zones. For the Chi-Town Alley footprint, I led the co-branding integration for "Fun Times Soda," seamlessly adapting their colorful, character-driven assets to fit the exhibit's gritty, street-inspired atmosphere through the use of brick texturing and graffiti-style treatments. I also developed the collateral for interactive attendee touchpoints, such as the Family Zone contest signage. By treating these distinct activations as extensions of the overarching environmental design, I ensured that partner brands and gamified giveaways felt fully integrated into the world-building rather than tacked on.
