Evolving a digital survey experience

Mack Trucks Survey Project (2015 Original vs. Modern Redesign)

  • Modern Redesign

    In this project I revisit the original 2015 online survey and reimagine how it could be improved using using modern user-centered design principles, UI/UX, interaction design, information architecture, contemporary visual design trends, and a stronger alignment with Mack's brand identity.

    Presented below as an 'Old vs. New' comparison, this project serves as a case study demonstrating my evolution and growth as a designer and my deeper understanding of modern digital product design.

    This comparison directly illustrates how I now approach similar challenges with an expanded skillset in areas like design thinking and creating more delightful, functional design systems, my ability to apply modern design thinking to significantly improve a user experience, and my commitment to creating high-quality, user-centric digital products.

  • About the 2015 Original

    While the original initiative was primarily focused on marketing goals, execution of the project involved significant underlying user experience challenges, including designing an intuitive user flow for survey completion, structuring information effectively, and ensuring a seamless experience across devices via responsive design.

    As the lead designer for the original survey site, I was responsible for:

    Design (UI/UX/Branding)

    • Translating the printed survey into an intuitive digital experience
    • Defining an online design language that was aligned to the campaign's print materials
    • Presenting my designs to cross-functional stakeholders

    Execution

    • Converting my mockups into a fast and seamless experience by writing minimal, optimized HTML/CSS code
    • Collaborating with the back-end team during the code phase to provide them with front-end code to which they could easily incorporate survey functionality

    Quality Assurance

    • Conducting a multi-stage quality control process to ensure accuracy
    • Reviewing user flow
    • Testing functionality and device compatibility

    The original campaign was highly successful, generating over 1,000 responders (qualified leads), with a significant percentage expressing interest in learning more or test driving a truck.

    This success, even within the context of 2015 design practices, highlights the impact of effective user flow and clear information architecture in meeting business objectives.
