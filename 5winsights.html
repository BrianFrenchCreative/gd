Implementing 5W’s new brand identity

Unifying the brand experience across tradeshow, marketing, and promotional materials

  • 🎯 The Challenge

    Following a comprehensive brand refresh, 5W's extensive library of tradeshow and marketing materials was completely misaligned with the new visual identity and strategic messaging. This fragmentation diluted the market impact of the launch and threatened brand consistency at critical customer-facing touchpoints.

  • 👤 My Role

    As the Creative Lead, I took full ownership of the visual transition—auditing the legacy inventory, engineering a scalable asset infrastructure, and executing the multi-channel rollout under tight deadlines.

  • ⚙️ My Process
    • Systemic Analysis: Conducted a deep-dive audit of all active collateral. I evaluated the gestalt of each touchpoint — from massive 3D tradeshow backdrops down to case studies, brochures, and digital ads — strategically deciding what required a rapid visual optimization versus what needed a ground-up redesign.
    • Scalable Execution: Moved rapidly into hands-on production, designing original graphic elements and integrating them into a robust, modular template system. This ensured that every single updated asset wasn't just visually stunning for the launch, but structurally engineered for future efficiency and effortless team reuse.
    • Stakeholder Collaboration: Partnered directly with marketing and executive leadership throughout the rollout, navigating shifting requirements and aligning cross-functional teams to ensure the final output flawlessly matched the new corporate direction.

  • 📈 The Outcome

    Delivered a flawless, cohesive suite of omnichannel marketing and experiential tradeshow materials that successfully projected a unified brand identity to the market. By transforming a fragmented legacy library into an agile system of scalable templates, I protected the brand's long-term governance and equipped the internal team with the tools to maintain visual consistency moving forward.
